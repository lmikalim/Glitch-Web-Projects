WHAT IS NOISE POLLUTION?

Noise pollution refers to the excessive or disturbing sound that interferes with normal activities, disrupts the balance of human or animal life, and can have harmful effects on physical and mental health. It is characterized by the presence of unwanted or unpleasant sounds in the environment, often arising from various sources such as traffic, industrial activities, construction, loud music, and other human-made activities. It's an issue plaguing over 100 million Americans

WHO DOES IT AFFECT?

Noise pollution is intertwined with environmental racism, as communities of color often face a disproportionate burden of environmental hazards. Discriminatory land-use policies place these communities near sources of noise pollution, contributing to health issues such as stress and sleep disturbances. Additionally, limited access to quiet spaces and insufficient representation in decision-making processes exacerbate the impact of noise pollution on marginalized populations, highlighting the intersectionality of environmental and social injustices. Addressing these disparities requires a comprehensive approach that considers community engagement, policy reforms, and the promotion of social and environmental justice.

WHAT DOES NOISE POLLUTION DO?

Noise pollution can have several adverse effects on both physical and mental health. These include:

Hearing Damage:

Prolonged exposure to high levels of noise can lead to permanent hearing damage or loss.

Sleep Disturbances:

Persistent noise, especially during nighttime, can disrupt sleep patterns, leading to insomnia and fatigue.

Increased Stress:

Continuous exposure to loud sounds can elevate stress levels, contributing to the release of stress hormones and potentially impacting cardiovascular health.

Impaired Cognitive Function:

Noise pollution has been associated with reduced concentration, impaired learning, and decreased cognitive performance.

Annoyance and Irritability:

Consistent exposure to unwanted noise can cause annoyance and irritability, affecting overall mood and well-being.

Interference with Communication:

Excessive noise can hinder effective communication, leading to misunderstandings and reduced social interactions.

Negative Impact on Wildlife:

Noise pollution can disturb natural habitats, affecting wildlife by interfering with communication, migration patterns, and breeding behaviors.

Health Issues in Children:

Children exposed to chronic noise may experience learning difficulties, impaired cognitive development, and behavioral issues. Addressing noise pollution is crucial to mitigate these adverse effects and promote healthier living environments.

DO I EXPERIENCE NOISE POLLUTION?

The picture above is a map of the noise pollution in my surrounding neighborhood.

This project was inspired by my own personal experiences with noise pollution. Back home in Chicago, I live in a predominantly working class Latino neighborhood that is next to a freight train yard, major highway, major commercial street, and most notably, Midway Airport. Noise pollution was one example of how environmental racism manifested my neighborhood.

I created this project to share a common experience that isn't often mentioned in the conversation of race-based and class-based violence. Noise pollution is a critical problem due to its significant impact on human health, environmental well-being, and overall quality of life. By raising awareness, communities can advocate for regulations, engage in urban planning, and encourage the development of quieter technologies, fostering more sustainable and livable environments. Addressing noise pollution is a shared responsibility that promotes better living for all.
